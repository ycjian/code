1. COMPANY OVERVIEW: What Snorkel AI Does & Why It Matters Now

Snorkel AI is one of the most strategically important companies in the current AI stack. Founded at Stanford, they pioneered programmatic data development — using code (labeling functions, weak supervision) instead of manual human annotation to create massive training datasets.

In 2025, they raised a $100M Series D and launched:
- Snorkel Flow: Core platform — turns raw enterprise data into curated training sets for LLMs.
- Snorkel Evaluate: Fine-grained, specialized model evaluation beyond generic LLM-as-a-Judge.
- Snorkel Expert Data-as-a-Service: A managed service combining their platform with a curated community of subject matter experts (PhDs, domain specialists).

The Core Business Insight: Generic foundation models (GPT-4, Claude) are not production-ready for specialized enterprise domains (medical, legal, financial). Snorkel helps enterprises fine-tune smaller, cheaper, more accurate models using proprietary data — without exposing sensitive data to external providers.

Why the "Expert Contributor Lifecycle" role exists right now:
The Expert Contributor community is their key competitive differentiator for the Data-as-a-Service business. Building the software systems that manage the lifecycle of thousands of expert contributors (recruitment, task assignment, quality scoring, payments, fraud detection) is a critical engineering challenge. This is essentially building a high-quality human-in-the-loop ML platform at scale.

2. THE ROLE: Expert Contributor Lifecycle — What You're Being Asked to Build

The "Expert Contributor Lifecycle" team owns the platform that:
1. Onboards and vets contributors (PhDs, domain experts) for AI data tasks.
2. Routes tasks intelligently to the right experts based on skill match and availability.
3. Scores quality of expert contributions vs. programmatic ground truth.
4. Manages payments and compliance (contractor payroll, 1099s, international compliance).
5. Detects fraud and gaming (contributors manipulating tasks to earn more).
6. Builds the feedback loop back into Snorkel Flow — expert signal improves the platform's labeling functions.

This is a platform/systems engineering + ML infrastructure role, not a pure ML modeling role. It requires building resilient, high-throughput distributed systems that coordinate people and models.

4. CONNECTING YOUR ADOBE EXPERIENCE TO THE ROLE

A. Quality Scoring → Expert Contribution Scoring

Adobe: You built an LLM-as-a-Judge pipeline scoring 5M+ AI responses/day for safety, factuality, and artifact quality at <300ms p95 latency on AWS EKS.
Snorkel: Expert contributors produce data annotations. The platform needs to score contributor output quality against programmatic ground truth and detect when a contributor is gaming the task.
Bridge: "My eval pipeline at Adobe is architecturally identical to scoring expert contributions. Both require deterministic, scalable, low-latency quality assessment of human-or-AI-generated outputs. The scoring rubrics differ, but the infrastructure doesn't."

B. Canary Rollouts & Model Registries → Expert Quality Gating

Adobe: You implemented staged canary rollouts (1%→5%→25%→100%) with automated rollback on quality regression using Jensen-Shannon Divergence against shadow traffic.
Snorkel: Before promoting an expert contributor to high-value/high-trust tasks, the system needs a confidence score on their quality — exactly the same "gate before full promotion" problem.

C. Multi-Tenant Platform → Multi-Client Expert Routing

Adobe: You built multi-tenant isolation (AQL bitmask pre-filtering, per-tenant rate limiting, tenant-aware vector namespaces) for thousands of enterprise clients.
Snorkel: Expert contributors work across multiple client data projects. A contributor working on a pharmaceutical client's data must be isolated from a fintech client's sensitive annotations. Tenant-aware routing is required.

D. Rate Limiting & Backpressure → Task Queue Throttling

Adobe: You implemented Redis-based token bucket rate limiting and KEDA-based Kafka backpressure-aware task queues to manage GPU cluster saturation.
Snorkel: The Expert Contributor platform needs intelligent task dispatch — don't flood contributors with tasks faster than they can complete them; saturating experts leads to quality drops and churn. This is the same backpressure problem, applied to human workers instead of GPU nodes.

E. 50B+ Asset Search Platform → Expert-to-Task Matching

Adobe: You built hybrid retrieval (Dense + Sparse + RRF) to match queries to the most relevant documents across 50B assets.
Snorkel: Routing an incoming data task to the best-matched expert (by domain expertise, past performance, language, time zone, and availability) is a retrieval/ranking problem. Your search infrastructure experience directly applies.

5. PROBABLE RECRUITER QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

Q: "What interests you in Snorkel specifically?"

A: "Two things. First, the intellectual problem: building a platform that manages expert contributors — routing tasks, scoring quality, building feedback loops back into training data — is a genuinely hard distributed systems and ML problem. It's not building another chatbot. Second, the timing: Snorkel is positioned at the critical bottleneck of the entire LLM industry. Every enterprise trying to fine-tune a custom model needs high-quality, specialized training data. Snorkel is building the infrastructure that makes that possible at scale. I want to work on infrastructure that the rest of the AI industry depends on."

Q: "Why are you leaving Adobe?"

A: "Adobe is a fantastic company and I built some of the most technically challenging systems of my career there. But I'm looking for a company in the earlier, faster-growth phase where I can have a more direct impact on product direction and where I'm closer to the technical frontier of AI infrastructure. Snorkel's focus on data-centric AI and the Expert Contributor platform is an engineering domain I genuinely want to specialize in."

Q: "Have you worked with programmatic labeling or weak supervision before?"

A: "Not Snorkel Flow directly. But my LLM-as-a-Judge framework at Adobe is fundamentally a form of programmatic labeling — instead of manual human annotation, we use an LLM with structured rubrics to programmatically label AI outputs at scale at 5M annotations/day. The challenge of ensuring those programmatic labels are calibrated against human ground truth is exactly the weak supervision aggregation problem Snorkel solves. I understand the core intellectual problem deeply, even if I used different tooling."

Q: "What does 'Senior' mean to you?"

A: "Senior means owning not just implementation but the technical definition of a problem. At Adobe, I didn't just write the code for our eval pipeline — I defined what metrics mattered, designed the LLM-as-a-Judge rubric, built the canary testing gate to validate new models before they hit production, and presented the architecture to VPs. Senior means the output is a system other engineers can trust, not just code that runs."

6. TECHNICAL TOPICS TO BE READY TO DISCUSS (For Any Follow-up Technical Screen)

The role will likely probe these areas:
- Task queue design: How do you build a fair, fraud-resistant task assignment system for thousands of concurrent contributors?
- Quality scoring at scale: How do you programmatically measure if a human's annotation is correct without a perfect ground truth?
- Fraud detection: How do you detect a contributor clicking through tasks randomly to collect payment without doing real work?
- Ranking and routing: How do you match tasks to the best-qualified contributor given skill, availability, and historical quality?
- Payment/compliance systems: Contractor payroll, international tax compliance, 1099 automation.

Prepare short answers (2-3 sentences) for each of these based on analogous Adobe systems.
