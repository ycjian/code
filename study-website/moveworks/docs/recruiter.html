Auth0 / Okta Recruiter Call Prep - Emily (Senior Software Engineer, AI Agentic Experience)

1. Elevator Pitch (Introduce Yourself & Your "Why")

"Hi Emily, great to meet you. I’m currently a Senior AI Platform Engineer and Tech Lead at Adobe. My bread and butter is building large-scale ML and AI platforms from the ground up and taking them to production. Most recently, I’ve been leading the backend orchestration for conversational AI and agentic workflows across Creative Cloud and Document Cloud—specifically managing RAG, tool-calling, and retrieval at scale.

When I saw the 'AI Agentic Experience' role at Auth0, it stood out to me immediately. I already spend my days building the 'glue' that turns LLMs into autonomous agents, and applying that exact technology to the identity and security sector—which is such a critical and rapidly evolving domain—sounds like an incredible technical challenge. I’m a big fan of Auth0's developer-first approach, and I’m excited to learn how you're weaving agentic AI into that ecosystem."

2. Key Resume Highlights to Map to the Role

  • Agentic AI & Orchestration (The Core Fit): Emphasize your direct experience building the orchestration layer for RAG and tool-calling for Acrobat AI Assistant. You understand how agents plan, retrieve context, and execute actions, backed by robust caching and circuit breakers.
  • Evaluation & Guardrails (Crucial for Identity): Auth0 is an identity and security company; they cannot afford AI hallucinations in authorization flows. Highlight your Adobe work building LLM-as-a-judge pipelines, safety scoring, and fallback runbooks. Show that you think about "Trust & Safety" and deterministic guardrails natively.
  • Production Scale & MLOps: Identity systems require absolute reliability. Bring up your <300ms latency on EKS handling 5M+ evals/day, and your background at Amazon/Microsoft maintaining 99.9%+ uptime on high-throughput streaming pipelines.

3. Market & Domain Talking Points (To show you understand the landscape)

  • Generative AI in Identity/Security: Acknowledge that the market is shifting from "AI as a chatbot" to "AI as an autonomous agent." In the IAM (Identity and Access Management) space, agentic AI has massive potential for:
    • Automated Threat Remediation: Agents that can detect anomalous behaviors and automatically adjust authentication requirements (e.g., triggering MFA or step-up auth dynamically).
    • Developer Experience: Auth0 is famous for its DX. Agentic AI could drastically simplify how developers configure complex identity flows, policy writing, or integrate SSO.
    • Dynamic Authorization: Moving beyond static RBAC/ABAC to context-aware, agent-driven access policies.
  • The Shift to Multi-Agent Systems: Note that the industry is moving towards specialized, multi-agent orchestrations rather than single monolithic LLM calls. Your experience in orchestration positions you perfectly to build scalable agent topologies.

4. Questions to Ask Emily (To show enthusiasm and evaluate fit)

  • Team Scope & Vision:
    • "Can you share more about the specific mission of the 'AI Agentic Experience' team? Is the focus more on assisting developers who use Auth0, or building agentic capabilities directly into the end-user identity flows?"
    • "Is this a zero-to-one initiative, or are there already established AI products this role will scale?"
  • The Tech Domain:
    • "Identity demands incredibly high reliability and low latency. How is the team balancing the inherent latency of LLMs with Auth0's strict performance SLAs?"
    • "What does the AI stack look like currently? Are you heavily leveraging managed foundational models, or doing in-house fine-tuning and hosting?"
  • Logistics & Next Steps:
    • "What is the expected interview loop for this role?"
    • "Is the team heavily distributed, or based around a specific hub?"

5. Quick Reminders for the Call

  • Keep it High-Level: Emily is looking for alignment on skills, scale, and culture. Save the deep dive on reciprocal rank fusion for the technical screen. Focus on the business impact and the complexity of the systems you've built.
  • Emphasize "Secure by Design": Because it's Okta/Auth0, casually demonstrating that you think about data privacy, multi-tenant isolation (which you did at Adobe!), and system guardrails will score major points.
  • Be Ready for Compensation/Logistics: Know your salary expectations and confirm your location status and work authorization (U.S. Citizen).

ServiceNow (MMM) Recruiter Call Prep - Jacoby Lacey (Machine Learning Engineer, Search Platform)

1. Elevator Pitch (Introduce Yourself & Your "Why")

"Hi Jacoby, great to connect. I’m currently a Senior AI Platform Engineer and Tech Lead at Adobe, but prior to that, I spent four years specifically as an ML Engineer on the Search & Discovery team managing a multi-modal unified search platform for over 50 billion assets. In my current role, I build the backend orchestration and guardrails for Acrobat AI Assistant—so essentially enterprise conversational AI and RAG at scale.

When you reached out about the Search Platform role on the MMM team, it felt like an exact match for my trajectory. I’ve built both the dense/sparse hybrid retrieval systems (the core of traditional ML search) and the generative orchestration layers that turn search into automated tasks and answers. I’m incredibly excited about ServiceNow acquiring MMM to build the 'front door of work' because that's exactly the intersection of enterprise search and agentic AI I specialize in."

2. Key Resume Highlights to Map to the Role

  • Deep Search & Retrieval Background: MMM is fundamentally an enterprise search/retrieval problem underneath. Highlight your experience building hybrid retrieval systems (dense and sparse search in parallel, reciprocal rank fusion), query understanding (intent classification, entity expansion), which you built at Adobe.
  • Conversational AI / RAG at Scale: MMM' goal is to "get answers all in one place" and "automate tasks." Mention your role in building the Acrobat AI Assistant for Q&A, summarization, and citations. Highlight your work implementing RAG, tool-calling (for task automation), rate limits, and circuit breakers.
  • Enterprise Scale & Security (Guardrails): ServiceNow serves the largest enterprises. Emphasize that you didn't just build POCs—you led the implementation of LLM-as-a-judge, safety scoring, multi-tenant isolation, and scaling policies on EKS handling 5M+ evals/day.

3. Market & Domain Talking Points (Enterprise AI / "Front Door of Work")

  • Intranet/Enterprise Search is Hard: Acknowledge that enterprise search (the MMM domain) is one of the hardest retrieval problems due to fragmented data sources, permission complexities (multi-tenancy), and lack of click-data compared to consumer search.
  • From Search to "Action": MMM' vision isn't just returning links; it's resolving IT tickets and HR requests natively. Talk about how your experience with LLM tool-calling positions you perfectly to securely map natural language to internal APIs.
  • The GenAI / Traditional ML Synergy: Emphasize that GenAI doesn't replace traditional search but enhances it. Your background combines the classic ML ranking (neural reranking, feature-based relevance) with modern GenAI (semantic embeddings, LLM orchestration) which is exactly what a mature platform like MMM needs.

4. Questions to Ask Jacoby (To show enthusiasm and evaluate fit)

  • The Integration / MMM Context:
    • "How is the MMM team integrating with the broader ServiceNow infrastructure following the acquisition? Is the goal to replace the existing ServiceNow search, or build a specialized intelligence layer across the top?"
    • "What are the biggest scalability or retrieval challenges the team is facing right now as they try to consolidate enterprise knowledge into one platform?"
  • The Tech Stack & Role:
    • "Is the role focused more on foundational search latency and ranking algorithms, or on the generative orchestration and task-automation side of the stack?"
    • "What does the data infrastructure look like for training or fine-tuning models on specific enterprise domains?"
  • Logistics & Next Steps:
    • "What does the interview process typically look like for an MLE role on this team?"

5. Quick Reminders for the Call

  • Connect the Two Halves of Your Resume: Your last two roles (Search MLE + AI Platform Tech Lead) essentially equal the MMM product offering. Make sure Jacoby understands you possess both halves of the coin.
  • Focus on Business Impact: Emphasize how your caching and optimizations reduced latency by 30-50%, and your relevance models improved metrics by 25%. Recruiters love quantifiable impact.

Advanced Deep-Dive Talking Points (For Follow-ups & Technical Screens)

Auth0 / Okta: "Identity meets Agentic AI"

If Emily asks for a specific technical challenge you solved, point to:
* Rate Limiting & Circuit Breakers (Acrobat AI Assistant): Attackers often try to DDoS LLM endpoints to rack up API costs or break the service. Highlight your work implementing robust rate limiting and circuit breakers. In the IAM world, this translates directly to preventing credential stuffing or API abuse.
* Cost & Latency Observability: LLM APIs are expensive. Talk about your shared cost/latency dashboards. Auth0 needs to know you can build AI features that are economically viable at scale.
* LLM-as-a-Judge for Sensitive Data: Talk about how you shipped guardrails (safety and artifact scoring). For Auth0, an LLM misrouting access permissions is catastrophic. Showing you know how to build deterministic evaluation pipelines for non-deterministic models is a huge differentiator.

ServiceNow / MMM: "The Enterprise Knowledge Graph"

If Jacoby wants to go deeper into your ML/Search background, bring up:
* Handling the "Sparse vs. Dense" Problem: MMM deals with very domain-specific vocabulary (internal acronyms, proprietary code names). Point out your use of hybrid retrieval (dense + sparse with reciprocal rank fusion). Dense embeddings are great for general semantics, but sparse (BM25) is necessary for exact keyword matching of those internal acronyms.
* Multi-Modal & 50B+ Scale: MMM isn't just text; it's searching through Confluence pages, JIRA tickets, and Slack threads. Compare this to your multi-modal search at Adobe (images, documents, videos). The scale you handled (50 Billion assets) proves you can handle any enterprise's data volume.
* RAG with Multi-Tenant Isolation: Enterprise platforms cannot accidentally leak data between different clients (or different departments within a client). Remind them that your AI Platform architecture at Adobe included strict multi-tenant isolation and per-stage observability, which is mandatory for ServiceNow's B2B model.
